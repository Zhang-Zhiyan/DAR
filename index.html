Benchmarking Dynamic Affective Reasoning:
A Viewer-Centric Video Emotion Dataset

Moving video emotion understanding beyond static labels—toward temporally grounded, causal reasoning about how a viewer’s affect evolves.

1University of Science and Technology of China   2Hefei University of Technology

What is DAR?

A benchmark for when, what, and why a viewer feels.

DAR treats a video as a stream of event-aligned affective states instead of a single static label. The benchmark jointly evaluates temporal boundaries, 27 viewer-centric emotion categories, and causal explanations grounded in visible evidence.

Abstract

Dynamic affect is cumulative, event-driven, and temporally grounded.

Video emotion analysis is commonly formulated as static classification, treating each clip as an independent labeled unit. This overlooks a key psychological fact: emotions evolve through cumulative reactions to consecutive causal events. We introduce DAR (Dynamic Affective Reasoning), a large-scale benchmark designed to model these dynamics. DAR contains 15,087 videos and 36,908 event-aligned affective segments annotated with 27 emotion categories, together with dense temporal grounding and causal reasoning chains.

On this benchmark, we define affective segmentation, fine-grained emotion classification, and affective reasoning. We further propose DAR-R1, a two-stage framework that combines supervised fine-tuning with Group Relative Policy Optimization, establishing strong performance in both temporal localization and viewer-centric reasoning.

Dataset

Large-scale, viewer-centric, and event-aligned.

118.8words / rationale

Rich causal explanations rather than short captions.

Three benchmark tasks

  1. 01
    Affective segmentation

    Locate the temporal boundaries where a viewer’s emotional state changes.

  2. 02
    Fine-grained emotion classification

    Recognize a 27-category viewer-centric affective state for each segment.

  3. 03
    Affective reasoning

    Explain why visible events induce that emotion, while maintaining temporal context.

Data split

13,646training videos
1,441test videos

Construction follows a coarse-to-fine pipeline: semantic boundary proposals are refined with visual cuts; differential captions reduce redundancy; and stream-of-affect reasoning is checked with dual-consistency verification.

Method

DAR-R1: adapt the format first, then refine the reasoning.

Stage 1

Cold-start supervised fine-tuning

Adapt a general MLLM to produce valid structured outputs: segment timestamps, emotion labels, and causal rationales.

Stage 2

GRPO-based reinforcement learning

Optimize boundary precision, segment count, emotion correctness, structural validity, and grounded reasoning quality without a separate critic.

Output

Temporally grounded affective reasoning

For each emotional phase, DAR-R1 returns start, end, emotion, and reason.
